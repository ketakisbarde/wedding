Our Story

The Adventure of a Lifetime

8,000+ miles, 3 time zones, and infinite chai

The Beginning
Aamchi Mumbai

Two dreamers from the city of dreams. Local trains, monsoon rains, vada pav at every corner, and the endless shimmer of Marine Drive at dusk.

Raised on Mumbai's chaos, charm, and cutting chai at Marine Drive!

Chapter One — BOM ✈️ ROC
Jab We Met

Two Mumbai kids crossed oceans to chase something bigger. Grad school at RIT wasn’t just about degrees — it was about becoming. From barista shifts to TA hours, learning to cook dal for the first time, late-night study sessions that turned into deep conversations, house parties in the snow, building bonds that would last forever, and navigating a brand new world… we grew into the people we were always meant to be. Strong. Independent. And, somewhere in the middle of all of it — friends.

We came for a degree. We left with something we didn’t know we needed — each other.

Chapter Two — 2,100 Miles Apart
Across the Timezones

He moved to San Francisco. She moved to Atlanta. 2,100 miles apart — but the distance only pulled them closer. Weekend FaceTime marathons turned into cross-country flights. Pani Puri competitions at Chai Pani, scooter rides down the Beltline, Coca-Cola tastings at World of Coke — every visit felt like home away from home. Date nights that started as “just catching up” turned into something neither could ignore. Somewhere between the airport hugs and the goodbye tears, they realized they were way more than friends — and finally said it out loud.

Two cities, three timezones, a hundred flights — and a love that refused to stay long-distance

Chapter Three — SEA
A Ferry Ride in Seattle

They met in the middle — Seattle. First date on a ferry, Space Needle behind them, Mt. Rainier in the distance, the Great Wheel spinning. Unforgettable — especially because Ash lost the car keys. 🔑😅

A ferry ride & a Ferris wheel — first date. Legendary key-loss included.

Together at Last — SFO
The Everythingship

No more boarding passes. No more countdowns. Golden Gate sunrises, Twin Peaks sunsets, hikes through the Presidio, concerts at Chase Center, beach picnics at Pacifica, badminton on Tuesdays, and the kind of everyday magic you only find when you’ve finally found your person.

"My heart's been borrowed and yours has been blue — all's well that ends well to end up with you."

The Question
Sunset at Carmel Beach

A drive down Highway 1 — cliffs, cypress trees, and Carmel's white sand glowing gold. A beach picnic, a golden sunset, waves whispering secrets, and one very nervous question. She said yes before he finished asking.

"Yes. A thousand times yes." — The sky blushed. So did she.
